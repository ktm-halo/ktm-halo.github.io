    Introduction

    For this assignment, I decided to review Rich Brown's personal portfolio website, RichBrown.info. The site showcases a range of interactive design projects that highlight both his creativity and professional skill. In this reflection, I’ll share my experience exploring the website—looking at its navigation, animations, and overall engagement—to better understand how interactive features can effectively communicate design ideas.

    First Impressions & Actions

    First thing noticed

    The first thing I noticed was the clean and minimal layout, combined with bold typography and subtle animations. This immediately conveyed a sense of professional design and set the tone for the interactive experience.

    Discrete actions in two minutes

    • Scrolled through the homepage to understand overall layout
    • Clicked on several showcased projects to explore details
    • Read project descriptions and viewed accompanying images and videos
    • Explored animations and transitions on different pages
    • Interacted with the two interactive characters “R” and “B”
    • Visited the About and Contact sections

    Most time spent engaging

    I spent most of my time exploring the animations and interactive elements of each project, appreciating how motion and layout worked together to showcase creativity.

    Most common action

    I frequently clicked on the “R” character in the top-left corner, which functions as a back-to-home button, repeatedly returning to the homepage after viewing each project.

    Goals & Communication

    Impression of primary goal

    The primary goal of the website appears to be showcasing Rich Brown's design portfolio and interactive projects, highlighting both creativity and professional experience.

    How the goal is communicated

    This is achieved through interactive elements, clean and intuitive design, clear navigation, engaging animations, and the inclusion of images and videos for each project, which together demonstrate Rich Brown's design skills firsthand.

    Interaction Over Time

    Impression of how it should be interacted with over time

    In my opinion, each visit to the website is likely intended to be short, around five minutes, to quickly browse a few projects or check the site’s functionality. However, users are encouraged to return multiple times to explore projects in more detail and to see new updates over time.

    How the experience communicates this

    The site communicates this through preview images and summaries for each project, allowing users to quickly scan and decide which projects to explore further. The variety of projects, combined with unique interactive elements on each page, encourages multiple visits and gradual exploration over time.

    References, Feelings & Evaluation

    Other media forms referenced

    The site references other digital media forms such as portfolio galleries, interactive animations, and case studies, which are typical in UX/UI design presentations.

    How this affects user actions

    These references suggest that users should interact deliberately, clicking through individual projects while paying close attention to graphic details, animations, and overall site structure.

    How this affects user feelings

    The experience communicates a sense of curiosity and engagement, motivating me to explore and fully appreciate the creativity and thoughtfulness behind each project.

    Most frustrating part

    Some animations take slightly longer to load, which can disrupt the browsing flow. Additionally, certain interactive details require the user to guess how to interact, which enhances aesthetic value but may reduce immediate usability.

    Most satisfying part

    The smooth motion, clear transitions, and animated presentation of images and videos are the most satisfying elements. They create a visually pleasing and engaging experience, making the interactive exploration enjoyable and memorable.
